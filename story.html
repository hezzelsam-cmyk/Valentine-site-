My Heart to You

Alice,

I’ve taken time to sit with everything not from anger, not from desperation, but from a place of reflection. I don’t want this to be noise or emotion speaking over itself. I want this to be something you can read slowly and truly understand my heart.

Part of me will probably always be confused about why you chose this path. And another part of me genuinely understands that you were protecting yourself. I know you. I know how deeply you feel. I know how your mind sometimes runs ahead trying to prevent pain before it ever reaches you. I saw it in the way you would shut down or pull away when something triggered old wounds. I never saw you as dramatic or difficult I saw you as someone who simply never wanted to relive certain hurts again.

And that’s why this hurts the way it does. Because what you thought was happening and what was actually in my heart were two completely different things.

Those screenshots I sent between me and Tina are the ones that form the real story, some of them being from the day I was coming kwako — kindly look at them and see what I say to her, the parts you didn’t see. From the start, I was upfront with Tina: I told her I was speaking from a place of pain, not interest, that I was still in love with you, and that I was trying to fix what we had and do it right.

The same day I removed her out of respect for you because your feelings mattered more than anything else. I wasn’t hiding you. I wasn’t splitting my loyalty. I was choosing you. She suggested keeping communication “light and respectful,” and I made the mistake of believing that would be enough. My gut told me to end it completely, and I didn’t listen. I regret that deeply.

There’s even a message where I told her clearly that the moment we were officially back, I didn’t care what she felt or thought — she would be blocked, because your feelings mattered more to me than anyone else’s. That was always my position.

But where I truly failed was in the moment you saw everything and got hurt. Instead of immediately focusing on removing every doubt from your heart, I got caught trying to explain myself. I should have been quicker to reassure you, quicker to shut everything down completely, quicker to protect your sense of security. Even if I knew my intentions were clean, I should have understood that what you needed wasn’t explanation first — it was certainty.

My intention was never to entertain something else. But I understand now that intention doesn’t erase impact. Even if my heart was still fully with you, the situation created insecurity — and that matters.

I didn’t just see you as someone for now. I saw you as someone I wanted to build a future with. In time, I wanted us to create a family — not just any family, but one that didn’t break apart. One built on stability, understanding, and growth. I wanted us to experience that part of life together in a way we didn’t get to as kids. Not out of fear, not out of pressure, but out of choice and commitment.

I looked at you with love and long-term intention. Not temporary feelings, not convenience — but commitment. I never saw you through lust or as someone to pass time with. You were never a momentary desire to me. You were someone I respected deeply, someone I imagined building with, growing with, standing beside years from now. I wasn’t thinking about leaving when things got hard. I was thinking about staying and working through them.

Loving you wasn’t just about the big moments or the loud feelings. It was in the quiet details.

I noticed the small things most people would miss — the way you’d rub your thumb when you were nervous, how you’d pull at your sleeves when something was bothering you, how a slight shift in your tone meant your mind was racing, how your silence sometimes said more than words ever could. I learned when you needed reassurance, when you needed space, and when you just needed someone to sit with you without trying to fix anything.

I didn’t pay attention because I had to. I did it because you mattered to me. Because understanding you felt natural.

I kept mental notes of your dreams, the places you casually mentioned wanting to visit, the foods you liked, the random ideas you’d say in passing. Not to impress you — but because they were pieces of you. And loving you meant valuing all of it, even the smallest details.

What we shared wasn’t ordinary to me.

It was the late nights laughing until we couldn’t breathe. The inside jokes no one else would understand. The teasing. The comfort of just existing beside each other without needing to perform. The long drives where we talked about everything and nothing at the same time. The way we tried, even when we didn’t always get it right.

Those moments were real to me. They mattered.

I regret the moments where I reacted instead of listening. I regret not always recognizing when you needed reassurance instead of explanation. If I could redo anything, it wouldn’t be to change who we were it would be to handle certain moments with more care and maturity.

I’m not writing this to pressure you. I’m writing it because what we had deserves honesty.

Whatever you decide, I hope you remember that my love for you was real, intentional, and never temporary. And if our paths don’t meet again, I will still be grateful that I got to experience something that deep with you.
